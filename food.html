Food in New York City

In New York City, there are many restaurants which you can visit during your trip to this city, on this page there is information about the top 10 restaurants in New York City which will cover many meals during your time in New York City.

1. Katz's Delicatessen

Katz's Delicatessen is rated as one of the most famous New York City restaurants, it has been serving sandwiches since 1888. There is only one reason why you should eat at Katz's Delicatessen and that is for the famous pastrami that they put on their sandwiches. It has been reviewed as 'Everything you want in Pastrami they provide'.

Address of Katz's Delicatessen: 205 Euston St, New York, NY 10002, USA. Open Hours: 8am - 10:45pm on Monday – Wednesday, 8 am – 2:45 am on Thursday, 8 am – all night on Friday, open all day on Saturday, 8 am – 10:45 pm on Sunday.

2. Spicy Village

Spicy Village is a tiny restaurant located in Chinatown in Manhattan that are known for specializing in home cooked style dishes from China. If you are a spicy food fanatic and in New York City you have to try this restaurant then you cannot get any better than the Spicy Big Tray of Chicken. Spicy Village has been listed on many restaurants because the flavors and friendliness of this little restaurant are standout.

Address: 68 Forsyth St #B, New York, NY 10002, USA. Open hours: 10 am – 11 pm on Monday – Saturday (closed on Sunday)

3. Di Fara Pizza

When you are in New York there are many pizza places that you will get told to try but one which is obscure but highly recommended is Di Fara Pizzas in Brooklyn. This is a small family run restaurant owned by pizza making legend Domenico DeMarco. DeMarco says that his pizzas taste so good is because the ingredients of cheese and tomato come straight from Italy. Definitely a place to eat whilst in New York City.

Address: 1424 Avenue J, Brooklyn, NY 11230, USA. Open hours: 12 pm – 8 pm on Tuesday – Sunday (closed on Mondays)

4. The Halal Guys

The Halal Guys is not a resturant, it is a food cart and is one of the original New York City street food carts. Their food is Middle Eastern inspired and they serve Halal food as their name shows. The part of the food which amkes them famous is their white and red sauce, it tranforms a plain dish without it into an incredible with the red chilli sauce and the white sauce.

Address: W 53rd St, New York, NY 10019, USA. Open hours: 10 am – 4 am daily, one of the late night restaurants in NYC when you need a huge meal at 3 am.

5. Russ and Daughters

Another New York City heritage staple is Russ and Daughters, a deli with beginnings as a street food pushcart and has been appetizing since 1914. Russ and Daughters is one of the most famous New York City restaurants for serving all types of salmon, both smoked and salt cured, with bagels and cream cheese.

Address: 179 E Houston St, New York, NY 10002, USA. Open hours: 8 am – 8 pm on Monday – Friday, 8 am – 7 pm on Saturday, and 8 am – 5:30 pm on Sunday.

6. Meske and Ethiopian

Along with more famous New York City restaurants that have become institutions in the city, NYC also provides you with the opportunity to sample cuisines from around the world and one of them restaurants is the Meske and Ethiopian restaurant. Meske serves excellent Ethiopian food and is one of the restaurants in NYC in this guide that also has many vegetarian options.

Address: 468 W 47th St, New York, NY 10036, USA. Open hours: 12 noon – 10:30 pm daily.

7. Shake Shack

When it comes to the best burger chain in the United States, New York’s Shake Shack has been leading a burger revolution and wowing burger fanatics recently. The menu includes burgers, hot dogs, and shakes, and I decided to go straight for the Double Shack Burger with a side order of fries. The burger meat is made up of short rib, sirloin, and brisket which is always fresh and never frozen. The bun is a potato roll which is very soft and toasted in Butter before being compiled.

Address: Madison Ave and E.23rd St, New York, NY 10010, USAOpen hours: 11 am – 11 pm daily

8. Gloria's Caribbean Cruise

Gloria’s Caribbean Cuisine, located in Crown Heights, Brooklyn, serves a mix of Caribbean food, most of which is Trinidadian. For incredibly delicious curries and friendly service, Gloria’s Caribbean Cuisine is one of the restaurants in NYC you don’t want to miss.

Address: 764 Nostrand Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11216, USA Open hours: 11 am – 9 pm on Tuesday – Thursday, 11 am – 9:30 pm on Friday and Saturday, and 11 am – 8:30 pm on Sunday and Monday.

9. Xi'an Famous Foods

With multiple locations around New York City, Xi’an Famous Foods specializes in serving flavors from Xi’an, a city located in the northwestern part of China. It is located in a shopping mall which feels like you have travelled from New York City to China.

Address: Golden Shopping Mall, 41-28 Main St Basement, Suite 36, Flushing, NY 11355. Open hours: 10:30 am 9:15 on Sunday to Thursday and 10:30 am – 9:30 pm on Saturday and Sunday

10. Los Tacos No.1

If you’re looking for some of the best tacos New York City has to offer, and possibly equally as good as tacos elsewhere in the United States, Los Tacos No.1 will satisfy your every need.

Address: Chelsea Market, 75 9th Ave, New York, NY 10011, USA. Open hours: 11 am – 9 pm from Monday – Saturday, 11 am – 8 pm on Sunday
