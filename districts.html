Districts in New York City

New York City is a city of great scale and within it, New York City holds all the districts that people will be living in and working in.

New York City has a total of 5 districts and information about them all can be found on this page.

Brooklyn

Brooklyn is one of the five boroughs that New York City holds, it is located on the southeast side of New York City. It is the most populated borough of New York City with around 2.6 million residents with as many as 200 different languages, these incredible stats do not make it the biggest borough geographically as Queens is the biggest borough. Brooklyn is home to the oldest building in New York City, the Wyckoff House Musuem and has the amazing Brooklyn Bridge for you to explore.

Queens

As stated above, Queens is the largest borough geographically in New York City. If it were to split away from New York City it would be the 4th largest city in the United States of America, it has an incredible 2.3 million residents which is 400,000 less people than all of Chicago. Queens is home to both of the airports when you fly into New York City, it has the LaGuardia Airport and John F. Kennedy Airport (JFK). Queens is connected to the Bronx by three bridges: the Bronx Whitestone Bridge, the Throgs Neck Bridge and the Triborough Bridge. It is home to the Flushing Meadows Corona Park which is a public park which has the area of 3.63km squared.

Manhattan

Manhattan is the centre of the New York Metropolitan Area and is the smallest borough in New York but however is the most densely populated borough. Manhatten Island is the home of two boroughs, Manhattan and the Bronx. Central Park in Manhattan became the first landscaped public park in an American City. New York City has 468 subway stations and there are 147 in Manhattan so that's just over a third of subway stations are housed in Manhattan. As mentioned above Manhattan houses Central Park but also houses the famous street of Wall Street and the incredible skyscraper that is the Empire State Building.

The Bronx

The Bronx is the northern part of New York City. The Bronx area is home to the New York Yankees, the Bronx Zoo and the New York Botanical Garden. The Bronx is the third most densely populated county in the United States but about a quarter of its area is open space. It has a population of 1.455 million people. The Bronx is a borough which has 13 colleges and universities. The area includes a collection of neighborhoods including Bronx River, Harding Park, Soundview-Bruckner and Soundview.

Staten Island

Staten Island is the last borough. Staten Islanders refer to the borough they live in as 'The Island'. Staten Island is the forgotten borough but it is the richest borough in New York City by having an average income of $70,295. Staten Island used to be the place for people to vacation and hang out during the summer. People would be all over the beach and in their vacation houses by the water. There would be carnivals, parks and people all over just spending time in the suburbs of New York City. Staten Island is the third largest borough in NYC, but the least populated.
